# Swarm lane 27

## Technician Agent: Scanner Equipment Calibration

Lane 27 is responsible for calibrating scanner equipment used in 3D specimen capture operations. The technician agent in this lane performs systematic calibration procedures to establish baseline accuracy specifications and identify drift corrections needed for reliable measurement data.

## Calibration Workflow

The technician agent executes the following calibration sequence:

1. **Equipment Initialization**: Power up scanner hardware and verify all optical and mechanical components are operational.
2. **Reference Measurement**: Capture baseline scans of calibration artifacts with known dimensions and material properties.
3. **Accuracy Assessment**: Compare measured values against reference specifications to quantify systematic and random errors.
4. **Drift Analysis**: Identify temperature-dependent drift, mechanical wear, and optical degradation patterns.
5. **Correction Factor Calculation**: Compute correction matrices and adjustment coefficients for each measurement axis.
6. **Report Generation**: Produce calibration_report containing instrument accuracy specs and drift corrections.

## Output: Calibration Report

The calibration_report artifact contains:
- **Instrument Accuracy Specifications**: Precision metrics for each measurement axis (X, Y, Z)
- **Drift Corrections**: Time-dependent and temperature-dependent adjustment factors
- **Confidence Intervals**: Statistical bounds for measurement reliability
- **Audit Trail**: Timestamp records for compliance and traceability

## Integration Points

- **Feeds to Lane 03 (Validation)**: Calibration data adjusts measurement confidence intervals for downstream validation processes
- **Stored in Lane 10 (DynamoDB)**: Calibration reports persisted with timestamps for historical audit trail
- **Audit Trail in Lane 31**: Calibration events logged for compliance and equipment maintenance scheduling

## Technician Responsibilities

The technician agent must:
- Follow manufacturer calibration protocols precisely
- Document environmental conditions during calibration (temperature, humidity, vibration)
- Validate calibration results meet minimum accuracy thresholds
- Escalate equipment failures or out-of-spec conditions to maintenance
- Maintain calibration schedule compliance for regulatory requirements
