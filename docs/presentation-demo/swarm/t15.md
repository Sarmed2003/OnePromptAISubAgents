# Swarm lane 15

## Curator Agent: Specimen Provenance Documentation

Lane 15 is responsible for documenting the complete provenance of fossil specimens, capturing critical metadata about collection date, excavation location, excavation notes, and storage history. This lane maintains an authoritative chain-of-custody record that ensures the scientific integrity and traceability of each specimen throughout its lifecycle.

## Input Contract

Lane 15 receives fossil records from lane 01 (Data Ingestion) containing basic specimen information and collection context.

## Processing

The curator agent processes incoming fossil records and enriches them with comprehensive provenance metadata, including:

- **Collection Date**: The precise date when the specimen was excavated or collected
- **Location**: Geographic coordinates and site information for the excavation location
- **Excavation Notes**: Detailed field notes and observations recorded during specimen collection
- **Storage History**: Complete record of how the specimen has been stored, transported, and handled since collection
- **Chain-of-Custody**: Metadata documenting every transfer and custodian of the specimen

## Output Contract

Lane 15 outputs a `provenance_record` containing all chain-of-custody metadata and provenance information. This record is persisted to DynamoDB (lane 10) for reliable long-term storage and retrieval.

## Downstream Consumption

The provenance metadata produced by lane 15 is consumed by two critical downstream lanes:

- **Lane 24 (Librarian)**: Uses provenance records for citation indexing, ensuring that specimens can be properly cited in scientific literature with complete attribution and source documentation
- **Lane 32 (Director)**: Uses provenance records for publication validation, verifying that specimens meet institutional standards and ethical requirements before they are referenced in official publications

## Data Persistence

All provenance records are stored in DynamoDB with appropriate indexing to support rapid lookup by specimen ID, collection date, location, and custodian history. This ensures that the complete chain-of-custody can be reconstructed and verified at any point in time.
