# Swarm lane 22

## Conservator Agent: Preservation Treatment Records

Lane 22 operates as the conservator agent within the fossil curation swarm, responsible for logging and managing specimen preservation treatment records. This lane processes fossil records from lane 01 (input) and generates conservation metadata that documents all conservation methods, chemical treatments, and storage conditions applied to specimens throughout their lifecycle.

## Data Flow and Integration

The conservator agent receives fossil records as input and maintains a comprehensive conservation_log stored in DynamoDB (lane 10) with full timestamp tracking. Each treatment record captures the conservation method employed, specific chemical treatments applied, and detailed storage condition parameters to ensure specimen integrity and compliance with preservation standards.

## Output and Consumption

The conservation_metadata output from lane 22 is consumed by two critical downstream lanes:

- **Lane 15 (Curator)**: Uses conservation metadata for provenance tracking and complete specimen history documentation
- **Lane 31 (Auditor)**: Leverages treatment records for comprehensive data integrity checks and compliance verification

## Key Responsibilities

The conservator agent maintains the complete treatment history of each specimen, ensuring that all preservation interventions are properly documented, timestamped, and accessible for downstream curation and audit workflows. This systematic approach guarantees traceability and accountability in the fossil preservation process.
