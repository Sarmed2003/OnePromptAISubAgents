# Swarm lane 12

## Velociraptor Claw Morphology Cataloging

Lane 12 executes a specialized research agent workflow that catalogs Velociraptor claw morphology in parallel with the T-Rex anatomical analysis conducted in lanes 01–05. This lane follows the established fossil records → validation → anatomical modeling schema to produce the velociraptor_anatomy_dataset output.

## Workflow Overview

The Velociraptor claw morphology cataloging process operates through the following stages:

1. **Fossil Record Ingestion**: The researcher agent collects and normalizes Velociraptor claw fossil data from paleontological repositories, extracting morphological measurements and structural characteristics.

2. **Validation Layer**: Collected data undergoes rigorous validation against established anatomical parameters, ensuring consistency and accuracy of claw morphology measurements including curvature, size, and material composition.

3. **Anatomical Modeling**: Validated data feeds into 3D anatomical modeling systems that reconstruct Velociraptor claw geometry and functional properties based on fossil evidence.

## Data Integration

The lane references the canonical species definition at `dinolab/web/src/data/species/velociraptor.ts` to maintain consistency with the broader species database. Output from this lane—the velociraptor_anatomy_dataset—serves as a critical input for lane 14 (symmetry analysis), enabling cross-species morphological comparison between Velociraptor and T-Rex claw structures.

## Agent Responsibilities

The researcher agent in lane 12 is responsible for:
- Systematic cataloging of Velociraptor claw specimens
- Extraction and normalization of morphological features
- Quality assurance of anatomical measurements
- Generation of structured dataset output compatible with downstream analysis pipelines
