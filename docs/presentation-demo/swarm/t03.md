# Swarm lane 03

## Research Assistant: Measurement Validation

Lane 03 validates fossil measurements from lane 02 against statistical norms and instrument calibration records. This stage ensures data quality and reliability before downstream analysis by the anatomist and biologist teams.

## Input

**Source**: Skeletal data with measurements from lane 02 (fossil preparation and measurement)

**Format**: Measurement dataset containing:
- Individual measurements (bone dimensions, angles, ratios)
- Measurement metadata (instrument type, calibration date, operator)
- Raw measurement values with units

## Validation Process

The research assistant performs the following validation checks:

1. **Statistical Norm Validation**: Compares each measurement against established statistical norms for the species and specimen category. Identifies measurements that fall outside expected ranges.

2. **Instrument Calibration Review**: Cross-references measurements with calibration records to ensure instruments were properly calibrated at the time of measurement. Flags any measurements taken with out-of-calibration instruments.

3. **Outlier Detection**: Applies statistical methods to identify potential outliers and measurement errors. Calculates confidence intervals for each measurement.

4. **Data Quality Assessment**: Evaluates measurement precision, consistency across repeated measurements, and operator competency indicators.

## Output

**Format**: measurement_validation_report

**Contents**:
- Validation status for each measurement (pass/flag/fail)
- Outlier flags with justification
- Confidence intervals (typically 95% CI)
- Instrument calibration status
- Statistical deviation from norms
- Recommendations for data use or remeasurement

## Downstream Integration

The validation report feeds into lanes 04 and 05:
- **Lane 04 (Anatomist)**: Uses validated measurements and confidence intervals for morphological comparison and phylogenetic analysis
- **Lane 05 (Biologist)**: Incorporates validated measurements into biomechanical and functional morphology modeling

Measurements flagged as outliers or failing validation are clearly marked, allowing downstream researchers to make informed decisions about data inclusion.
