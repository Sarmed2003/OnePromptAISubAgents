# Swarm lane 13

## Archivist Agent: Pteranodon Wing Data Indexing

Lane 13 is responsible for indexing comprehensive Pteranodon wing morphology data from the species track, extracting wing-specific anatomical features including attachment points, joint ranges, and structural characteristics. This indexing produces the `pteranodon_anatomy_dataset` output, which serves as a specialized reference for comparative anatomical analysis.

## Data Source

The Archivist agent processes wing anatomy information from `dinolab/web/src/data/species/pteranodon.ts`, extracting detailed morphological measurements and structural parameters specific to Pteranodon wing anatomy.

## Output Dataset

The `pteranodon_anatomy_dataset` generated by this lane contains structured wing morphology data with the following key components:

- **Attachment Points**: Precise anatomical coordinates where wings connect to the torso and limbs
- **Joint Ranges**: Quantified ranges of motion for wing joints, including flexion, extension, and rotation parameters
- **Structural Morphology**: Wing shape characteristics, bone density distributions, and membrane attachment patterns

## Downstream Consumption

The indexed Pteranodon wing data is consumed by:

- **Lane 14 (Symmetry Analysis)**: Compares bilateral wing symmetry and validates structural balance in wing morphology
- **Lane 19 (Illustrator)**: Generates comparative anatomical charts and visual representations of Pteranodon wing structure relative to other pterosaur species

## Integration Notes

The Archivist agent in lane 13 operates independently on the Pteranodon species track, ensuring specialized and detailed wing anatomy indexing that maintains data integrity for downstream comparative analysis and visualization tasks.
