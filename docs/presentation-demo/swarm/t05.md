# Swarm lane 05

## Biologist Agent: Muscle Attachment Point Analysis

Lane 05 is responsible for analyzing muscle attachment points on the validated skeleton from lane 03 and the rigged model from lane 04. The biologist agent processes biomechanical data to identify precise attachment locations and compute force vectors that represent muscle-bone interactions.

## Input Contract

This lane receives two primary inputs:

- **validation_report** from lane 03: A comprehensive skeleton validation report containing joint positions, bone geometry, and structural integrity checks.
- **rig_model** from lane 04: The rigged character model with skeletal hierarchy, control structures, and deformation weights.

## Processing Pipeline

The biologist agent performs the following analysis steps:

1. **Attachment Point Identification**: Scans the validated skeleton to locate anatomically-accurate muscle attachment sites on bone surfaces, using reference data from biomechanical literature and validated anatomical models.

2. **Force Vector Computation**: Calculates directional force vectors for each muscle attachment, representing the mechanical forces that muscles exert during contraction and movement.

3. **Constraint Generation**: Derives biomechanical constraints that enforce realistic muscle behavior, including attachment point stability, force magnitude limits, and anatomical range restrictions.

4. **Validation Against Rig**: Cross-references attachment points with the rig model to ensure compatibility with deformation weights and skeletal control structures.

## Output Contract

This lane produces **muscle_data** containing:

- **attachment_points**: A mapping of muscle identifiers to 3D coordinates on the skeleton, with associated bone references and local surface coordinates.
- **force_vectors**: Directional vectors representing muscle pull directions, magnitude constraints, and temporal dynamics.
- **biomechanical_constraints**: A set of rules and limits that ensure physically plausible muscle behavior during animation and simulation.

## Downstream Dependencies

- **Lane 06 (Rendering)**: Consumes muscle_data to visualize muscle geometry and attachment points in the rendered output.
- **Lane 14 (Symmetry Analysis)**: Uses attachment_points to verify bilateral symmetry and ensure consistent muscle behavior across left-right body pairs.

## Key Responsibilities

The biologist agent must ensure that all attachment points are anatomically grounded, force vectors are biomechanically sound, and constraints preserve the integrity of both the skeleton and the rigged model. This lane acts as a critical bridge between structural validation and both visual rendering and symmetry verification.
